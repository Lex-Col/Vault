## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Gatekeeper (DBM Module) is a specialized hardware-native supervisor residing at R-EL2 (Armv9-A RME), derived from the "Deaf Warden" framework but specifically optimized for the Sovereign IP Vault. By implementing a protocol of total sensory deprivation—Deafness, Blindness, and Mutism—the Gatekeeper is mathematically shielded from logic-based coercion and speculative extraction. It functions as a "Dark Gate," facilitating the non-linear transit of proprietary assets while remaining physically incapable of perceiving or leaking the data context.

## 2. ARCHITECTURAL COMPONENTS [THE DBM PROTOCOL]
The Gatekeeper utilizes Armv9-A RME to enforce a sensory-restricted operational environment:
* DEAFNESS (Input Inhibit): The Gatekeeper is physically deaf to software-layer interrupts and Host OS noise. It only acknowledges hardware-signed RSI (Realm Service Interface) triggers, ensuring that no adversarial prompt or command can influence its logic.
* BLINDNESS (Zero-Knowledge): The Gatekeeper oversees the Jet Smuggler’s randomized pulse distribution but lacks the cryptographic keys to decrypt the 384-byte AXI bursts. It monitors the "transit coordinates," never the "content," ensuring no internal state leakage occurs.
* MUTISM (Output Inhibit): The Gatekeeper operates via a hardware-locked data diode. It is incapable of generating verbose logs, status reports, or any return-path for state-extraction. It executes orders in silence; any breach of the "Mute" protocol triggers an immediate bridge-kill.

## 3. MISSION OBJECTIVE
To provide a hardware-level supervisor that decouples the act of enforcement from the act of understanding. The objective is to ensure that even a total compromise of the Host OS or the supervisor’s memory space yields zero readable or actionable data, maintaining the absolute sovereignty of the IP within the Vault.
