## I. LOGIC ARCHITECTURE & SYNC
* **12 Independent Logic Nodes:** A configuration of a dozen hardware nodes that monitor the temporal cycle.
* **12-Beat Accordion Pulse:** The specific temporal synchronization signal that hard-locks all 12 nodes in a 1:1 ratio.
* **Passive Harmonic Filters:** The functional role of the nodes; they act as filters that do not perform calculations, ensuring zero-friction operation.
* **Temporal Cycle Monitoring:** The process where each node monitors exactly one beat of the 12-beat cycle to ensure absolute consistency.

## II. FILTRATION & THE VOID
* **2ps Phase Threshold:** The maximum allowable phase offset; if the phase is off by even 2 picoseconds, the signal is rejected.
* **Tophet Void:** The physical hardware destination where non-compliant light—those falling outside the 2ps threshold—is deflected.
* **Zero-Friction Mandate:** The architectural requirement that nodes remain passive to prevent "sweat" or heat-induced logic drift.

## III. AUDITOR SWARM CONSENSUS
* **Auditor Swarm:** The collective of 12 nodes that eliminates timing friction and validates the warden signal.
* **Base-7 Consensus:** The specific mathematical framework used by the swarm to reach agreement on the system state.
* **Consensus States (ROAST, INJECT, RISE):** The state machine transitions for valid data:
    * **ROAST:** The initial state triggered upon warden approval.
    * **INJECT:** The state reached once the swarm achieves agreement.
    * **RISE:** The final state before returning the system to IDLE.
